Wanna explore the the Capital of the Nation, Yes I am talking about Dil walon ki Dilli!

Yeh Dilli h mere yaar I live in New Delhi, the capital of our incredible India. I was always fascinated by different cities where my friends lived until I knew that my birthplace Delhi is charming in it’s own way. Starting off with the fact that will astonish you much as it astonishes me is that Delhi and New Delhi are two different places altogether. New Delhi serves as the capital of the country and Delhi is more of a substantial city. Delhi is known to be a seat to three arms of the Government of India – Legislative, Executive and the Judiciary. Delhi has a long history of empire successions from the Delhi Sultanate to Mughals and then the British, the city at present stands as one of the fastest developing cities in the world. It’s loved both by the inhabitants and the tourists for it is called the city of the wise and the spice! Delhi has a never-ending list of fascinations it offers but if you visit Delhi these are the few must have experiences that will leave an impact in your mind and heart for the lifetime;
Places to see
From party places to monuments and ancient buildings, Delhi is blend of art and culture, modernisation and tradition, beauty and charisma. Few of the remarkable historical sites in Delhi are India Gate, Humayun’s Tomb, Red Fort, Qutub Minar, Agrasen ki Baoli, Firoz shah Kotla Fort, Purana Kila whereas some of the must visit temples in Delhi are Akshardham Temple, Lotus Temple, Jama Masjid, Gurudwara Bangla Sahib, ISKON Temple and many more. For all the shopaholics, Delhi is the home for some of the world famous markets. The famous bargain centres in Delhi are Janpath and Tibetan Market, Paharganj, Chandni Chowk, Sarojini Nagar and some of the rich markets are Khan Market, Dilli Haat, Sundar Nagar, Lajpat Nagar, Meena Bazar, and some special ones like Chor Bazar, Hand Book Market and Phool Mandi. One could not resist visiting these beauties in case they want a day full of shopping. Next comes the Signature Bridges of Delhi. It is cantilever spar cable-stayed bridge which spans the Yamuna river at Wazirabad section, connecting it to East Delhi. It is India’s First asymmetrical cable-stayed bridge making it one of it’s kind. This bridge is the tallest structure in Delhi and is almost the double the height of the Qutub Minar and now it has become a famous selfie point for the tourists and local people of Delhi. Delhi has a place for the animal lovers too. The National Zoological Park of Delhi is a famous place to be visited by the school children, families and animal enthusiasts. It has an enriched environment for the animals and it follows conservation education and sustainable management practices. It plays a major role in species preservation and reducing man animal conflicts in urban landscape. Bird Sanctuaries also come under this category.
Next comes the gardens of Delhi. Delhi comprises of several gardens and Botanical houses which again makes on the features that Delhi is famous for. The Garden of Five senses, the Lodi Gardens, the Buddha Jayanti Park etc are some famous gardens within Delhi. India Gate and Rajpath: the symbol of Patriotism and beauty are the memoir to the martyrs of the country. People come to see the cravings of the names of the soldiers on the wall of India Gate. Extending right from India Gate is Rajpath, a road that straight leads to the Rashtrapati Bhawan. The Mughal Garden has often been potrayed, and deservedly so, as the soul of the Rashtrpati Bhawan.It is also one the places to see in Delhi as it is opened once a year for people to explore hundreds and thousands of beautiful plant and tree species. It takes inspiration from the Mughal gardens of Jammu and Kashmir. It is really a pleasure for the eyes. Another gem of Delhi is the age-old market that goes by the name of Connaught Place. It was built by British and it has retained the charm over the years. The beauty lies in the way it’s constructed in concentric circles and the mains are the inner circle and the outer circle. These circles enclose a lush green garden with blossoming flowers where our national flag flare with pride. One can find essence of patriotism in every existing place of Delhi and that is true beauty of the capital of our country, Delhi.
Food to Eat
Delhi provides pleasure not only to your eyes and souls through all it’s beautiful sight seeing places but also your taste buds through it’s world famous delicacies. To start with the awesome street food we have have in Chandni Chowk. It is famous for marriage shopping and Parathas at the Paranthe Wali Gali, Jalebi at Delhi’s Old Famous Jalebi Wala. Other famous street foods are Pakode at Pakode Wali Gali, Lajpat Nagar 2 , Aaloo chaat at Prabhu Chaat Bhandar, Khan Market, and momos in almost all the local streets of Delhi. Some famous food points where people go with their families just to enjoy the lip smacking specialities are Mutton Korma at Karim’s, Jama Masjid, Sambhar, Idli and Masala Dosa at Saravana Bhawan and Paan at Odeon Paan Palace, Connaught Place, Sweet Lassi at Bikanerwala, Khan Market, Chole Bhature at Chache Di Hatti, Kamla Nagar. One cannot get over with the food options available in Delhi. Some go to places for teenagers with their friends are Cha Bar for tea, Keventers for milkshakes, numerous cafes in Hudson Lane and Champa Gali, Saket, Restaurants in Model Town and Chanakya Puri etc. These places are always filled with loads of youngsters having gala time. From Designated food stalls and restaurants to unlimited availability of food at every corner of the street, one can never get over the list of food options in Delhi. I can never sit and eat at home of I have guests at home, I ought to take them to a new place than before to eat and explore.
Now if we talk about spices which are a main component of anything we eat, Delhi has the largest market of spices in Asia. The Khari Baori market, established in the 17th century, still holds its reputation to date. It is situated very close to the Fatehpuri Masjid. This market has a fantastic fragrance spread across the area and it attracts the tourist and the city dwellers to its doorstep. Coming next is the largest wholesale market of fruits and vegetables, which is again in Delhi. Along with spices market, Delhi also occupies the largest wholesale market of fruits and vegetables in Asia. The Azadpur market is approximately around 80 acres and that is huge. One can never stay afar from food. Savouring oneself with food especially if its cheap and tasty and healthy is the best thing one can do with themselves.
Experiences to remember
If someone comes to Delhi and explore, they can create memories and experiences of lifetime. There can hardly be something that Delhi doesn’t offer. Everything a person do in Delhi makes a person want even longer to stay here. Starting off with the fact that Delhi is home to the President of India, where India being the world’s largest democracy which epitomizes strength, its democratic traditions and secular character. If one visits Delhi, they can say they have seen the house of the President and the Prime Minister of the country, isn’t it incredible? Then comes the monumental beauty of Delhi, which can never escape from the hearts of the onlookers. The forts filled with historical essence and the temples along with their devotional beauty slays everyone with the peace and serenity they offer. Also, there is the Signature bridge across the river Yamuna, which is known to be the tallest structure in Delhi and India’s first asymmetrical cable-stayed bridge, so why would someone not remember it?
Then there are markets providing anything one could possibly ask for, at best prices. Food at every crossroads. A person in Delhi can say that they are in the leading commercial center of India. We have talked about the places on can visit but what about the transportation facilities in Delhi. Well, they are one to be talked about. Delhi has the 13th largest metro rail network in the world. It makes almost every part of Delhi easily approachable. Whereas the Delhi Transport Corporation along with other transport services in Delhi entirely run on Compressed Natural Gas (CNG), making it substantial for the local people and for the visitors. Another wonderful experience one could get in Delhi is that it is the second most bird-rich capital in the world. The Delhi ridge inhabits a large number of bird species. This can be witnessed in the one of most famous bird sanctuaries of Delhi the Okhla Bird Sanctuary. To relive the charm of the British Era one can visit Connought Place. It is one of the most important landmarks of Delhi. Going to India Gate and Roaming around the empty roads in Connught Place is total fun. Last but not the least the beautiful gardens of Delhi will make you stay in here forever. Staying in Delhi or visiting Delhi is itself an Experience to Remember.

Travelling is important, Chalo ghumne!

Travelling becomes a hobby and necessity for all age people. Youth are playing a vital and most important role for the area of travelling. People who are engaged with the business sector must travel on the regular basis to promote their business and trade and on other hand youth gives importance to travelling for the education and leisure. Travelling is now regarded as an important part of education and activities for youths.
Educational tours for young students increases their knowledge powers and gives new thoughts and ideas to grow up in them. It’s a true fact that the education and knowledge of man remain incomplete if he or she does not visit important places of the world. Reading of books only provides man with half knowledge. But travelling with eyes wide open and mindfully gives him full knowledge.
On other ways, travelling is the most important mean of gaining health, happiness, a pleasure. It also helps to develop national unity in youth. Travelling is mostly for pleasure as it exhilarates out the heart and broadens our mind. We find great pleasure in seeing the beauties of nature. The mighty mountains and the meadows, the streams and the river, the deep blue sky, the green trees, the grand desert and the vast sea all touch the heart of the traveller.
Travel also develops the youth and providing an opportunity to meet new people . Travelling makes us familiar with the vast world outside the narrow space of our homes. We meet many people belonging to various cultures. We start appreciating the tradition, manners and customs of other people. We often accept and introduce some of their good elements in our own culture.
