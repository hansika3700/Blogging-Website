A coffee a day, keeps a Grumpy away!

I believe in enjoying a cup of coffee. Stepping into the coffee shop and taking in a breath of fresh coffee stimulates my sense of smell to explore every scent around me. When I finally grasp my cup, I feel the coffee raise my heart rate making every pump of blood loud and clear. Even the bitter tasting cups, make my day brighter. Coffee never fails to stimulate my sense of smell, touch, and taste.
That slight moment of smelling the ground coffee beans awakens my sense of smell. The scent of coffee is almost bitter but the distinctive smell arouses my nose to explore every scent around me. I suddenly interpret a sweet aroma and observe the red rose decorating the small brown coffee table where I sit. The flower’s sweet smell makes me realize how much beauty this one flower’s scent adds to ambience of the small coffee shop. If flowers did not exist, the world would bare less character. Each and every cup of coffee I drink allows me to more fully enjoy even the smallest of scents flowers bless the world with every day.

Caffeine can affect your sleep by keeping you awake longer, thereby shortening the amount of sleep you get, and giving you less time in the restorative stages of sleep. This takes a toll on your level of alertness the next day and on overall health.
Interestingly, though, caffeine doesn’t affect the stages of sleep the way other stimulants do, so it’s a better choice than speed or other uppers if you need to stay awake.
There are many health benefits that come from drinking coffee. Coffee can overall make you a happier person. Drinking coffee can make a positive impact on your life. Does coffee really stunt your Growth? How does drinking coffee help in your day to day life? Coffee is loaded with antioxidants and beneficial nutrients that can improve your health. Studies show that coffee drinkers have a much lower risk of several serious diseases. Coffee can make you feel happier and reduce the rate of suicide. A study carried out by the National Institute of Health Study in the US found that those who drink four or more cups of coffee were about ten percent less likely to be depressed than people who never drink it.
